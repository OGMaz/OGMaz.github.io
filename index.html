Happy 2 Years Anniversary

Celebrating our anniversary would have been so much easier if distance weren’t an issue, but we’ve never let that stop us before. And even though I’m as broke as can be right now (Much like my girlfriend sometimes, my bank account’s giving me the silent treatment right now), I wanted to make this day meaningful in a special way. So instead of buying something, I decided to create something just for you—something out of the box to make this year memorable.

My original plan was to use the last of my money to get you flowers and plan a nice dinner, because what’s an anniversary without flowers? But as much as I’d happily spend every last penny on you, I also knew it wouldn’t be a good idea if it meant going without food for the next two weeks. So, I was stuck—until I remembered a video I saw a while back about a guy who programmed a website to ask his girlfriend to be his Valentine’s. And I thought… maybe that’s it. Now, baby, let’s be real—I am no IT expert, but I do know me a little programming, and I figured this could be the perfect idea.

Once the idea was there, all that was left was making it happen. For the past two weeks, I’ve been working on this project nonstop. And because I’m a perfectionist when it comes to you 😏, I wanted every little detail to be just right, which, of course, meant even more time and effort. But honestly? Doing this for you made all the work feel like fun. I just hope you can see how much thought and love I’ve put into this, even if I couldn’t get you a physical gift this year. You know, one of my biggest love languages is giving you things, and it really hurts that I couldn’t do that the way I wanted to. But I hope this shows you just how much you mean to me.

Yours, always
